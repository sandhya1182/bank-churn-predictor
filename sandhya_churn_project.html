01 — Project Overview

Why This Project Matters

Banks lose billions when customers leave. Acquiring a new customer costs 5–7× more than retaining one. This project builds a proactive ML system that flags at-risk customers weeks before they leave.

Key Contributions

→Complete end-to-end pipeline from raw data to business insights
→Class imbalance addressed using SMOTE (1,630 → 6,370 churned samples)
→3 ML models compared across 5 evaluation metrics
→Feature importance + SHAP analysis for explainability
→5 actionable business recommendations for bank management
→Interactive Power BI dashboard for non-technical stakeholders

Dataset Features (18)

CRISP-DM Methodology Pipeline

1
Data Collection

Kaggle Dataset — 10,000 records, 18 features

Bank Customer Churn dataset sourced from Kaggle. Verified shape (10000, 18), zero null values, target distribution of 79.62% stayed vs 20.38% churned.

2
Exploratory Data Analysis

12 Visualisations across all key features

Univariate and bivariate analysis revealing: 20.38% churn rate, Germany at 32.4%, complaint correlation at 99.5%, older customers churning more (44.8 vs 37.4 avg age).

3
Preprocessing

Encode · Scale · Drop identifiers

Dropped RowNumber, CustomerId, Surname. Label-encoded Gender, Geography, CardType. StandardScaler applied. Stratified 80/20 train-test split maintaining churn ratio.

4
SMOTE Balancing

1,630 churned → 6,370 (training only)

Applied SMOTE exclusively to training data. Balanced 50-50 training set of 12,740 records. Test set kept unbalanced for fair evaluation. Zero data leakage.

5
Model Training

Logistic Regression · Random Forest · XGBoost

Three classifiers trained on balanced data. All achieved 99.85% accuracy with only 3 total misclassifications on 2,000-record test set.

6
Insights

Feature Importance + Power BI Dashboard

Random Forest feature importance: Complain (0.782), Age (0.070), NumOfProducts (0.053). Interactive Power BI dashboard with KPI cards, slicers, geographic charts.

Key EDA Findings

Complaint–Churn Correlation
Near-perfect correlation. Customers with complaints churned at 99.5% — the dominant predictor in the model.
32.4%
Germany's Churn Rate
Nearly double France (16.2%) and Spain (16.7%). Suggests systemic dissatisfaction or strong fintech competition.
44.8
Average Age of Churned Customers
Churned customers are significantly older (44.8 yrs) vs retained (37.4 yrs). Age is the #2 predictor.
100%
Churn Rate with 4 Products
Every customer with 4 products churned. 3 products: 82.7% churn. Optimal is 2 products (lowest churn).
25.1%
Female Churn Rate
Female customers churn at 25.1% vs 16.5% for males. Statistically significant (chi-square p < 0.05).
26.9%
Inactive Member Churn
Inactive members churn at 26.9% vs active at 14.3% — nearly double. Inactivity is a strong early warning signal.

06 — Business Strategy

5 Actionable Recommendations

Data-backed strategies for bank management to reduce customer churn.

01
Prioritise Complaint Resolution
99.5% of complainants churn. Implement 24–48 hr resolution SLAs and proactive follow-up after closure.
02
Age-Targeted Engagement
Customers above 40 churn significantly more. Dedicated relationship managers and wealth-planning products are needed.
03
Germany Market Focus
32.4% churn is nearly double other markets. Localised retention campaigns and competitive benchmarking urgently required.
04
Optimise to 2 Products
3–4 products = 82–100% churn. Cap cross-selling at 2 products and redesign onboarding toward optimal bundling.
05
Re-engage Inactive Members
Inactive members churn at 26.9%. Auto-trigger re-engagement campaigns after 30–60 days of inactivity.
